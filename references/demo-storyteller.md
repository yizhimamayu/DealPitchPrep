# Demo-Storyteller: Future-State Vision & Demo Design

Designs a compelling future-state narrative and a concrete demo concept that
shows how the client's world improves after adopting the product. Uses a
dual-perspective structure: Human-Center (experience transformation) first,
Business-Center (outcome metrics) second.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Select the Anchor Scene + Case

From Value-Proofer output, pick one validated scene+case as the demo anchor.
Selection criteria:
- Highest total persuasion score (most bulletproof case)
- Broadest attendee appeal (touches multiple roles' interests)
- Most visual/emotionally compelling transformation story

This scene becomes the "hero narrative" for the demo.

---

### Step 2 — Identify the Core Beneficiary

Research the client's business to identify **who specifically** benefits from
the product. This is the human at the center of the story.

**Beneficiary types to consider**:

| Type | Example for Pos Indonesia | Example for SaaS company |
|------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| External consumer | A rural customer using Pospay for the first time | An end-user of the client's product |
| Frontline employee | A postal agent processing digital payments | A salesperson using the CRM |
| Operations staff | IT ops managing payment infrastructure | A data analyst running reports |
| Business partner | A merchant accepting QRIS via AgenPos | An integration partner |
| Internal decision-maker | The CCO reviewing real-time fraud alerts | The CFO seeing cost reduction dashboards |

**Research steps**:
1. Search the client's website, app store pages, and press releases to
   understand who their users are
2. Look for customer testimonials, user personas, or journey descriptions
3. Identify one primary beneficiary whose daily journey is most transformed
   by the product

**Deliver beneficiary profile**:
```
# Core Beneficiary Profile

**Name/Persona**: [Give them a name, e.g., "Budi, a 34-year-old"
  small shop owner in Surabaya"]
**Current situation**: [Their context, their goals, their constraints]
**Current pain**: [The specific moment where frustration peaks —
  be concrete and emotional]
**Relationship to client**: [How they interact with the client's
  product/service today]
```

---

### Step 3 — Human-Center: Journey Transformation

Map the beneficiary's **current journey** (with pain) and **future journey**
(with the product). Focus on emotional and experiential change — not
features, not metrics.

**Journey map structure**:

```
# Journey Transformation — [Beneficiary Name]

## Current Journey (WITH PAIN)

| Stage | What They Do | What They Feel | The Pain Point |
|-------|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| 1. Awareness | [Action] | [Emotion] | [Friction] |
| 2. Onboarding | [Action] | [Emotion] | [Friction] |
| 3. Daily Use | [Action] | [Emotion] | [Friction] |
| 4. Problem Resolution | [Action] | [Emotion] | [Friction] |
| 5. Growth/Expansion | [Action] | [Emotion] | [Friction] |

## Future Journey (WITH OUR PRODUCT)

| Stage | What They Do | What They Feel | The Improvement |
|-------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| 1. Awareness | [Action] | [Emotion — positive] | [How it's better] |
| 2. Onboarding | [Action] | [Emotion — positive] | [How it's better] |
| 3. Daily Use | [Action] | [Emotion — positive] | [How it's better] |
| 4. Problem Resolution | [Action] | [Emotion — positive] | [How it's better] |
| 5. Growth/Expansion | [Action] | [Emotion — positive] | [How it's better] |
```

**Writing rules for the journey**:
- Use sensory language: "Budi waits 3 minutes for the payment confirmation
  to appear, anxiously checking if the transaction went through" — not
  "Payment processing is slow"
- Name emotions: frustrated, anxious, relieved, delighted, confident
- Be specific about time, place, device, context
- Focus on moments of delight in the future journey — where does the
  product make them smile or sigh with relief?

**The "Why It's Better" narrative**:
After mapping both journeys, write 2-3 paragraphs answering:
> "Why does this change in experience matter to [Beneficiary]? Why do they
> CARE about this transformation?"

Connect the experience change to human values: time with family, pride in
work, sense of security, feeling of progress, dignity in service.

---

### Step 4 — Business-Center: Outcome Mapping

Now translate the experience transformation into business language that
every attendee in the room understands.

```
# Business Outcome Mapping

## Experience Change → Business Impact

| Experience Change | Business Outcome | Metric | Timeframe |
|-------------------|-----------------|--------|-----------|
| [Beneficiary feels X instead of Y] | [Business result] | [KPI] | [Expected time] |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |

## Cumulative Business Case
- Revenue impact: [$$ or %]
- Cost impact: [$$ or % saved]
- Efficiency impact: [time/process metric]
- Risk reduction: [compliance/security metric]
- Customer satisfaction impact: [NPS/CSAT metric]
```

**Connection rule**: Every business metric must trace back to a specific
human experience change. Do not claim "20% revenue increase" without
explaining WHICH beneficiary behavior change drives it.

---

### Step 5 — Demo Concept Design

Based on the journey transformation story, design one of two demo formats:

#### Option A: Simulated Product Demo

A live or pre-recorded walkthrough of the product in a scenario that mirrors
the beneficiary's journey. Designed to feel like "a day in the life" rather
than a feature tour.

**Design template**:
```
# Simulated Demo Concept — [Beneficiary Scenario]

## Demo Setup
- **Duration**: 3-5 minutes
- **Environment**: [Live product / Pre-recorded / Prototype]
- **Branding**: [Use client's logo, colors, real location names]
- **Data**: [Use realistic but fictional data matching client's context]

## Demo Script (Scene by Scene)

### Opening (0:00-0:30)
[What the screen shows — e.g., "Login screen with Pos Indonesia branding"]
[What the presenter says — set the context, introduce the beneficiary]

### Stage 1: [Journey Stage Name] (0:30-1:30)
[Screen action: clicks, transitions, animations]
[Presenter narration: connect to the pain point]
[Emotional beat: pause on the moment of relief/delight]

### Stage 2: [Journey Stage Name] (1:30-2:30)
[...]

### Stage 3: [Journey Stage Name] (2:30-3:30)
[...]

### Closing (3:30-4:00)
[Summary screen with key metrics visible]
[Transition to business outcomes — "This is what it means for [Client]"]

## Client Asset Integration Checklist
- [ ] Client logo on login/dashboard
- [ ] Client brand colors in UI
- [ ] Indonesian language / Rupiah currency
- [ ] Local context (e.g., Surabaya, Jakarta references)
- [ ] Realistic user data (anonymized Indonesian names)
- [ ] QRIS / local payment method integration visible
- [ ] Compliance badges (OJK, BI certifications)
```

#### Option B: Video Shoot Script

A short video (60-120 seconds) that tells the beneficiary's transformation
story cinematically. Can be shown in the meeting or shared as follow-up.

**Script template**:
```
# Video Script — "[Beneficiary Name]'s Day Changed"

## Format
- Duration: [60-120 seconds]
- Style: [Documentary / Cinematic / Split-screen before/after]
- Talent: [Actor / Animation / Screen recording + VO]
- Music: [Emotional arc: tense → hopeful → uplifting]

## Script

---
### SCENE 1: THE PAIN (0:00-0:20)
[Visual]: [Specific, relatable scene of the beneficiary struggling]
[Voiceover/Beneficiary]: "[Quote expressing frustration in their own words]"
[Music]: Tense, unresolved

### SCENE 2: THE TRANSITION (0:20-0:35)
[Visual]: [Moment of discovery — seeing the product for the first time]
[Voiceover/Presenter]: "[One line about the solution — not features, just the promise]"
[Music]: Shifts to hopeful

### SCENE 3: THE NEW REALITY (0:35-0:55)
[Visual]: [Same situations as Scene 1, but now smooth and joyful]
[Voiceover/Beneficiary]: "[Quote expressing relief/confidence — natural, not marketing]"
[On-screen text]: [Key metric: "3 min → 10 sec"] [Emotional beat: smile]
[Music]: Uplifting, resolved

### SCENE 4: THE BUSINESS IMPACT (0:55-1:10)
[Visual]: [Dashboard / metrics / team celebrating]
[Voiceover/Presenter]: "[One line connecting to business outcome]"
[On-screen text]: [Business metrics: revenue, efficiency, cost savings]
[Music]: Triumphant but not overblown

### CLOSING (1:10-1:20)
[Visual]: [Product logo + Client logo side by side]
[Text]: "[Client name] + [Your company]. Built for [beneficiary type]."
[CTA]: "See it live."
[Music]: Resolves to brand tone
---

## Production Notes
- [ ] Location: [Where to film / what backdrop]
- [ ] Cast: [Beneficiary actor profile — age, look, authentic]
- [ ] Props: [Devices, environment details]

```

[Journey transformation + "why they care" narrative]

## Key Principles

1. **Human first, business second**: Always start with the beneficiary's
   emotional journey. Business metrics without a human story feel hollow
   in a first meeting.
2. **Specific beats generic**: "Budi's shop in Surabaya" beats
   "a typical merchant." Real emotions beat abstract benefits.
3. **Demo is a story, not a tour**: Do not show features. Show a person's
   day getting better. Features are the means, not the message.
4. **Client assets = credibility**: Using their logo, their language,
   their currency, their context transforms a generic demo into "this
   was built for us."
5. **3-5 minutes max**: Attention collapses after 5 minutes in a first
   meeting. Every second must advance the story.
6. **Have a Plan B**: Demos fail. Always have screenshots or a recorded
   version ready.
